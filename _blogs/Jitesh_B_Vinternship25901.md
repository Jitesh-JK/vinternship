This quote was shared during the very first stand-up session by Prof. Sudarshan Iyengar, and at that moment, it felt motivating. Looking back now, it feels deeply personal.

When I began this internship, I honestly didn’t know what MERN even stood for. I wasn’t confident about where I was starting—but I decided to start anyway. Two weeks in, I can say this journey has changed how I look at learning.

The experience began on ViBe, a learning platform by the Vicharanashala Lab for Education Design (VLED). In the first stand-up, Prof. Sudarshan Sir mentioned that ViBe draws inspiration from Vikram–Betal. That idea stayed with me—learning here wouldn’t be passive. It would question you back.

The first few days tested patience. Due to heavy traffic, the platform had delays, and progress felt slow. But once things stabilized, learning became surprisingly engaging. The videos were short, focused, and addictive in the best way. Each one naturally pushed me to continue.

What kept me consistent was not just the content, but the subtle motivation built into the platform. The leaderboard, though not the goal, added a small push. That slight dopamine rush of moving ahead encouraged consistency. It wasn’t about competition—it was about moving forward.

As of now, I’ve completed over 80% of the ViBe course along with several case studies. Initially, I didn’t realize that case studies could be done in parallel with the course. Once I did, learning became more meaningful. Concepts learned through ViBe were immediately applied in real problems. The case studies didn’t just ask questions—they explained context, shared examples, and provided interactive coding spaces where we implemented and submitted solutions. It felt like learning by doing, not memorizing.

Beyond the technical learning, the stand-up sessions were energy boosters. Short quizzes, peer interactions, and breakout rooms helped build confidence, communication, and teamwork—skills that go beyond coding.

This journey was shaped deeply by the people behind it.
Prof. Sudarshan Iyengar’s guidance and motivating words gave direction.
Rohit Sir and Nitin Sir brought positivity and encouragement through every update.
Bhavna Ma’am and Meenakshi Ma’am ensured smooth coordination while staying actively connected with students.
Sakshi Ma’am consistently added a surprise element that kept the sessions lively and engaging.
And Nandan Sir, whose lectures we followed on ViBe, made the experience complete with his live session—openly addressing platform challenges and showing how student feedback directly influenced real improvements.

What stayed with me after the sessions wasn’t just MERN concepts or code—it was a mindset. The understanding that learning doesn’t require confidence at the start, only commitment. That consistency matters more than speed. That struggle is not failure—it’s progress in disguise.

Looking back, Eleanor Roosevelt’s words now feel personal. I started this internship thinking I couldn’t learn MERN, couldn’t keep up, couldn’t do justice to the journey. Yet, step by step, I did.

And that, perhaps, is the most valuable lesson this experience gave me.

Grateful to Prof. Sudarshan Iyengar Sir, Vicharanashala Lab for Education Design (VLED), IIT Ropar, NPTEL, and every mentor who made this learning journey possible.
